No contact decision

Should you write to your ex, or keep it unsent?

When the urge to write feels urgent, use this page to separate a real need from a moment of pain.

Write it without sending Check your streak

Write privately first

Wanting to write to your ex does not mean you have to send the message. Writing can be useful when it helps you understand the trigger, say the honest thing, and move the feeling out of your head. Sending is a different decision.

If the message is really about loneliness, panic, checking whether they still care, or trying to get one more answer, keep it private for now. Use the unsent letter prompts or the texting urge pause instead.

Send only if the reason is practical

A practical message has a clear purpose: shared bills, housing, belongings, children, pets, safety, or time-sensitive logistics. It should be short, specific, and easy to answer. If you need them to comfort you, explain the breakup, or prove they miss you, the message is probably not practical.

The calm checklist before sending

  1. Can I accept no reply without sending another message?
  2. Would this message still matter tomorrow?
  3. Is it about logistics, not reassurance?
  4. Is it shorter than five sentences?
  5. Does sending this protect my healing more than waiting?

If any answer is unclear, wait. Unclear usually means the feeling needs more time, not a faster send button.

If you want closure

Closure is understandable, but another message may give you a new loop instead of an ending. Write the closure message privately first. If you still want to send it after a day, read what to text an ex and keep the message calm and accountable.

If you already wrote the message

Do not open the chat app yet. Put the message somewhere private, take a short walk, drink water, and open your no contact calculator. The goal is to make the decision from a steadier body.

Should I write a long letter to my ex?

A long letter can help you process, but it is usually better kept unsent during no contact. Long letters often carry needs that your ex may not be able to meet.

Should I write to my ex if I miss them?

Missing them is real, but it is not always a reason to contact them. Wait until the message is not driven by the strongest part of the urge.

What if my ex wrote to me first?

Pause before replying. If the message is practical, answer briefly. If it is emotional or unclear, use this guide on what to do if your ex texts you.

Fast rule

If you need a reply, wait

Send only when you can handle no response, a cold response, or an answer you do not want.
