Why tracking helps

A breakup can make time feel blurry. A no contact tracker turns the boundary into something visible: one day, seven days, thirty days, and then longer stretches where you can finally see that the wave passes.

How to use it

Enter your breakup date and last contact date on the homepage. The tracker calculates your breakup day count, no-contact streak, relationship length, check-ins, and recovery milestones locally in your browser.

If you only want a fast count, use the no contact calculator. If you want a fuller record, start the tracker and save daily check-ins when the urge to text shows up.

Use it before you text your ex

The best moment to open the tracker is before you open the chat. Check the streak, write the message in an unsent note, and wait until your body is calmer. If the urge is strong, use want to text your ex before sending anything.

What makes this different from a notes app?

A notes app can hold your thoughts, but a no contact tracker gives the breakup a simple recovery structure: streak, check-in, urge, unsent letter, memory vault, timeline, and milestone card. The point is not to obsess over the number. The point is to give yourself a visible pause before you reopen the conversation.

No contact after breakup: what the tracker makes clearer

After a breakup, the hardest part is usually not understanding the rule. It is remembering the rule during a lonely hour. The tracker makes three things concrete: when no contact started, what tends to trigger you, and which milestone you are protecting today. For a fuller rule guide, read no contact after breakup.

When to restart your no contact streak

Many people restart the streak after a direct message, phone call, emotional reply, or in-person conversation that reopens the breakup loop. If you checked social media, you may not restart the official count, but it is still worth logging as a trigger. For a quick reset plan, read what to do when you broke no contact.

Quick reset: If you already texted, do not send a second message to explain the first one. Log the trigger, choose the next clean start date, and use the tracker as a restart point.

Frequently asked questions

What is a no contact tracker?

A no contact tracker counts the time since your last contact and gives you a place to record urges, check-ins, letters, and milestones.

How do I track no contact days?

Enter your last contact date, then check the streak whenever you feel tempted to text. You can also use the no contact calculator for a quick count.

Should I restart after texting my ex?

If the message reopened the emotional loop, restart from that contact date. Do not send follow-up messages just because the streak changed.

Can I use it without downloading an app?

Yes. The tracker runs in your browser and does not require signup.

What should I track during no contact after a breakup?

Track your last contact date, urges, mood, triggers, unsent messages, and milestones. The goal is not perfect data. The goal is a pause before contact.

Is this the same as a no contact counter?

A counter gives you the number. This tracker gives you the number plus a place to record the emotional moments that usually lead to texting.
