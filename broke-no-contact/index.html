Broke no contact

You did not fail. You found a trigger.

Breaking no contact feels heavy, but the next step is not shame. The next step is a cleaner reset.

Stop the second spiral

The first message may already be sent. The real risk is turning that message into checking, explaining, waiting, arguing, or sending five more.

Reset in three steps

  1. Write down what triggered the message.
  2. Do not send follow-up explanations while emotional.
  3. Restart your no-contact count from the next clean day.

What you can learn

Most broken streaks have a pattern: loneliness, alcohol, birthdays, seeing a post, a dream, or wanting closure. Name it so you can protect against it next time.
