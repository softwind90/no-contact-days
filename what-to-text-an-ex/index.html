Texting an ex

What to text your ex: 12 calm examples and when not to send.

Choose a message for a real purpose, not just a painful moment. Use the checklist first, then adapt one of the practical examples without turning it into a breakup essay.

Use this filter before texting

  • Is it necessary? Logistics, money, housing, safety, or shared responsibilities can be valid.
  • Is it calm? If you are shaking, angry, lonely, or waiting for reassurance, wait.
  • Is it short? A clear text should not become a breakup essay.
  • Can you accept no reply? If no response would crush you, the text probably needs more time.

Texts that are usually okay

Practical texts should be short and specific. These examples solve a real problem or state a clear boundary without reopening the whole relationship.

12 text examples by purpose

Logistics and shared belongings

“I found your keys. I can leave them with the front desk by 6. No need to meet.”

“Can you confirm the utility bill amount by Friday? I’ll send my share after that.”

“I’ll collect the remaining boxes Saturday between 10 and 11. Please let me know if that time does not work.”

“The pet appointment is Tuesday at 3. I’ll send the receipt afterward.”

A brief apology without asking for a reply

“I have reflected on how I handled that conversation. I’m sorry for raising my voice. I do not expect a reply.”

“I’m sorry I shared something private after we broke up. It was not respectful, and I will not repeat it.”

Setting or protecting a boundary

“I need space and will not be available for personal conversations right now. Please contact me only about the lease.”

“I’m not ready to be friends. I’m going to step back from contact so I can heal.”

“Please stop contacting me outside the parenting schedule. I will respond to messages about the children.”

Responding without reopening the breakup

“I received your message. I’m not ready to talk about the relationship, but I wish you well.”

“Thank you for checking in. I’m keeping the space I asked for and won’t continue this conversation.”

“I can answer the practical question, but I’m not available to revisit the breakup.”

Texts to avoid during no contact

Avoid texts that ask for emotional proof: "Do you miss me?", "Was any of it real?", "I just need closure", or "Can we talk one last time?" These can feel urgent, but they often restart the loop you are trying to leave.

Also avoid fake emergencies, jealousy posts, “accidental” messages, repeated apologies, or vague bait such as “I just heard something about you.” A message is not calm if its real purpose is to force a reaction.

If you want to apologize

An apology should be accountable and brief. Do not use it to demand forgiveness, restart the conversation, or make them comfort you. If you are not ready to send it without needing a reply, write it as an unsent letter first.

If you are in no contact

If the message is not necessary, protect the streak. Use the no contact calculator to see how far you have come, then put the message into the texting urge pause.

What is a safe first text to an ex?

A safe text is short, practical, and does not demand emotional labor. If there is no practical reason, waiting is often safer.

Should I text my ex for closure?

Only if you can handle no reply or an answer you do not like. If you need the reply to feel okay, wait and write the message privately first.

What should I not text my ex?

Avoid guilt, jealousy, vague bait, emotional essays, repeated apologies, and questions designed to make them prove they still care.

How long should I wait before texting?

There is no universal deadline. Wait until the purpose is clear, your body is calm, and you can accept no response. During no contact, keep communication limited to necessary exceptions.
