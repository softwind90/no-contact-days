This page is designed to be the fast tool you open when you want to know where you stand. Add your last contact date once, count the streak, then decide from the number instead of from the urge.

Last contact usually means the last time you texted, called, replied, reacted, or had a direct conversation with your ex. The result is not a score. It is a visible boundary you can come back to when your emotions get loud.

How to use the counter

  1. Enter the last day you directly contacted or replied to your ex.
  2. Use YYYY-MM-DD or YYYYMMDD format.
  3. Read the streak as a checkpoint, not as pressure to prove anything.
  4. If you feel close to texting, open the texting urge pause before sending.

No contact counter vs no contact calculator

A no contact calculator gives you the number for today. A no contact counter helps you keep coming back to that number as your streak grows. You can use this page for both: calculate your current day, then open the no contact tracker if you want to save check-ins and milestones.

If you searched for a no contact counter, start by entering the last date you texted, called, replied, or had direct contact. The result tells you how many full days have passed since then.

What counts as last contact?

Use the date of your last direct contact: a text, call, voice note, reply, in-person conversation, or any message that reopened the breakup conversation. If you only looked at their profile, you may not restart the official count, but it is still worth tracking as a trigger.

What to do with your result

  • Day 1 to 3: focus on waiting through the first waves instead of explaining everything. Use want to text your ex if the urge spikes.
  • Day 7: treat the first week as a real milestone, even if you still miss them. Read the day 7 guide.
  • Day 14: review triggers and notice whether checking has replaced texting. Use the tracker if you need a daily log.
  • Day 30: use the milestone to review your clarity, not as automatic permission to reach out. Check the no contact rule after breakup before deciding.

After you calculate

If the number is low: do not judge yourself. Your first job is to avoid sending a second message just because the first few days feel unbearable.

If the number is growing: protect the streak with a visible routine. Open the tracker, write the urge down, and decide later.

If you are asking whether to text: use the pause page before opening the chat app.

If you broke no contact

One message does not mean the whole process is ruined. Name the trigger, avoid sending follow-up messages out of shame, and restart from the next clean choice. If you need a reset, read what to do when you broke no contact.

Frequently asked questions

What date should I enter?

Enter the last date you directly contacted or replied to your ex. If you are only counting the breakup itself, use the breakup days calculator instead.

Is this a no contact counter?

Yes. Enter your last contact date and the calculator works as a no contact counter for your current streak.

Is this different from a no contact tracker?

Yes. The calculator gives you today's number. The no contact tracker is better if you want to track urges, milestones, and repeated check-ins.

Should I text my ex after 30 days of no contact?

Not automatically. Day 30 is a checkpoint, not a rule that says you should reach out. If the message is mostly anxiety, wait and use the pause tool first.

Do I restart after checking their profile?

Not everyone restarts the official count for profile checking, but it can still hurt your recovery. If checking keeps pulling you back, set a separate boundary around social media.

What if my ex texts me first?

You do not have to reply immediately. Read what to do if your ex texts you during no contact before responding.
